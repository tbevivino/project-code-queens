History


Named in 1899 after English village of St. Albans, St. Albans was developed in the 1920s and 1930s. In the nineteenth-century, the area was farmland.St. Albans is a neighborhood of detached one- and two-family homes, with a mix of attached homes and small apartment buildings. Much of the housing was built in the 1920s and 1930s.

Famous People


Given the list of gifted public figures who once called it home, St. Albans has bragging rights, even by New York standards. But beyond their role as window dressing, these long gone luminaries among them Ella Fitzgerald, Count Basie, John Coltrane and Lena Horne who have helped provide a sense of communal identity.

Places To See


Roy Wilkins Park (Merrick Blvd and 119th Ave) can be a hectic scene of basketball, tennis, and handball in the summer. The Roy Wilkins Family Center (718-276-8686) has a pool, gym, and fitness classes. The park is home to Black Spectrum Theater and its socially-conscious theater, after-school programs, and concerts. Each summer, the park hosts Irie Jamboree with top reggae and dancehall acts. St. Albans Park (Merrick Blvd and 172nd St) is an oasis of green on the edge of Addisleigh Park.
